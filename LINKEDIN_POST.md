# Draft LinkedIn Post

I built a small AI-powered humanitarian analytics demo focused on Sudan.

The goal was not to build a full NGO platform in phase one. I wanted to show the potential: how AI-assisted analysis can help humanitarian teams turn displacement context and community feedback into faster operational insight.

What the dashboard demonstrates:

- A Sudan displacement and needs overview
- Local NLP classification of synthetic IDP feedback
- Priority scoring for humanitarian hotspots
- A simple scenario forecast for planning discussions
- An intervention queue for NGO program teams
- An AI Action Brief for each selected hotspot
- A branded dashboard experience with a clean in-app logo reveal animation

No paid APIs. No private data. No personally identifiable information.

The most important lesson for me: AI in humanitarian work should not be presented as a replacement for field expertise. It should be a decision-support layer that helps teams listen better, prioritize faster, and explain tradeoffs more transparently.

This is only a proof-of-concept, but it shows where the opportunity is.
